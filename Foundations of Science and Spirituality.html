Foundations of Science and Spirituality (FSS)

Course Summary

Modern science unleashed stunning discoveries in the fundamental laws of matter, life and their interactions.  Contemporary educational system encourages pedagogy based on a rational or scientific methodology. With validated proofs, science provided very good understanding into the reality. However, the key questions that need to be addressed are (a) can science provide a mature understanding of the reality (b) what does the philosophy of science convey with respect to its ability to provide greater understanding into the innate aspects of the reality (c) as an alternate paradigm, what is the role of science in the methodology and realizations associated to religion/spirituality and (d) does science have a complimentary role in formalizing the acceptance of spirituality. Given the fact that todays graduates do not study the philosophy of science, the Certificate Course on the Foundations of Science and Spirituality (FSS) will train a delegate to understand and realize the inherent connections between scientific and spiritual paradigms of the reality and thereby formalize upon a greater harmony between science and spirituality. As the most fundamental course in the subject matter of science-spirituality, the FSS course provides very useful realizations for furthering the application of science-spirituality synthesis framework in various fields of human engagement including Education, Scientific and Social Research, Career and Profession, Management and Philosophy. Given the universality of the taught subject matter, the FSS course remains as a very important node to gain needful intellectual maturity for fundamental and applied human endeavors into the reality and its formal application in all walks of lifes journey.

Background and Need

Contemporary trends in educational frameworks emphasize upon a multi-disciplinary pedagogy to understand the reality and thereby drive practical applications for the betterment of the humankinds existence and co-existence in the nature. Contemporary educational and technological pedagogies emphasize upon rationality as the most dominant paradigm for human understanding and realizations. Such an approach is detrimental to the spirit of multi-disciplinary engagement. Human personality development indices strongly posit towards philosophical and spiritual foundations of life. Skilling desired for accolades in professional endeavors necessitates upon personality factors grounded in philosophical overview and spiritual dimensions of life. To accommodate such a worldview, graduates need to harmonize their understanding of the reality from scientific, philosophical and spiritual dimensions and thereby pave the way for a harmonious thought process and engagement. Otherwise, the one sided and overly bent pedagogies can jeopardize peaceful existence for a prosperous and bright tomorrow in the midst of ethical conflicts.

On the other hand, it is a known fact that many graduates do not understand the intricacies between religion and spirituality. While spirituality advocates upon the transcending of religion through the firm acceptance of spiritual laws of the nature, todays graduates and professionals equate religious engagement with spirituality. Also, religious diversity is often considered to be a stumbling block for a harmonious thought process and engagement in the professional and social dimensions of life. Hence, to customize the harmony between science and spirituality, spirituality must be rightly understood as a mature framework to transcend religious views and formalize upon the synthesis of world religions.

Finally, the synthesis of science and spirituality is the most fascinating subject matter due to its unique ability to harmonize diversified human thought processes, methodologies and systems that stem out from such diversities. The role of scientific temper in fostering spiritual wisdom and the role of spirituality in scientific rationality must be thoroughly understood so as to formalize greater harmony and synchronization between science and spirituality.  Advocating upon the aspects and perspectives mentioned above, the online course on Foundations of Science and Spirituality (FSS) will provide a very useful educational framework for the peripheral and domain analysis of the methodologies adopted in science, religion and spirituality. Encouraging a deeper study of life from scientific, philosophical and spiritual dimensions of life, the FSS course aims to foster the integration of these diverse paradigms for a multi-disciplinary engagement in the field of human learning par excellence. Emphasizing upon epistemological and ontological issues associated to scientific, spiritual and synthesis frameworks, the course will allow a delegate to get systematically trained to explore philosophical foundations of the reality and their contextual applications. Thereby, graduates of the course will be able to extend their existing knowledge into novel dimensions of holism and reap upon the benefits of the holistic paradigm in their personal, professional and social spheres of life. A graduate upon successful completion of the course will have broader outlook on the fundamental and applied issues in life through an ensemble of the philosophical worldview and spiritual dimensions of life and their effective integration with the scientific rationality.

Course Syllabi

Scientific Methods: Elements of scientific method, Foundations of Classical and Quantum Physics, paradigm shift, epistemology and ontology of science, science beyond the science.

Spirituality: Elements and Characteristics of a Spiritual Process; Astika Vedic Philosophies and their Pramanas; Axioms of Bhagavata culture; Spiritual coherence of Vedanta and World religions; Epistemology and Ontology of Spirituality

Spirituality in World Religions: Need for distilled essence of religious principles; Common principles of world religions; Axioms of the Bhaktivedanta School as the distilled essence of world religions

Synthesis of Science and Spirituality: Six sigma of science-spirituality synthesis; Limitations of rational approach to understand the reality; method of inference; divinity inspired rational science; fine-tuned universe; intelligent design; modern scientific validity of empirical Vedanta; common goals of science and spirituality; Vedantic perspectives of science and spirituality synthesis.

Assignment submission and evaluation policy

  • For each subject, one home assignment would be released as google form document. The same shall be submitted online.
  • Course pdfs and audios would be shared to each registered delegate to solve the assignment questions. Course videos can be accessed through an email and would not be allowed to download.
  • Each exam paper has only Multiple Choice Questions (MCQ). The questions have been prepared based on taught content. There can be more than one answer for the MCQ.
  • Each registered student must secure 50% marks in each exam paper. Otherwise, student shall revisit the lecture content and redo the assignments.
